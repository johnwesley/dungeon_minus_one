## Game Premise
GAME PREMISE
The player arrives at a place that was never meant to be encountered directly.
Long ago, an ambitious organization began work on a vast underground system intended to preserve value, knowledge, and cultural artifacts indefinitely. The project was carefully planned, endlessly revised, and aggressively overdesigned. It was never completed.
Funding disappeared. Leadership changed hands and then vanished. The original purpose was reinterpreted until it no longer resembled itself. Surface access points were quietly abandoned. Subterranean systems were left active under the assumption that someone else would eventually take responsibility.

No one did.

Below ground remains a maze of stone corridors, traps, and chambers built to protect things no longer clearly defined. Automated defenses, custodians, and self-appointed guardians continue to perform their assigned roles with varying degrees of conviction. Some believe in the mission. Some reinterpret it. Some exploit it.
Above the ruins sits an unremarkable house, installed late in the project as an access structure. It was designed to look ordinary. It succeeded too well. Inside are a trophy case for tracking recovered assets and a trap door that was supposed to be temporary.

The player is not a hero.
The player is not authorized.
The player is simply present.

The task remains unchanged: explore what lies below, recover items of residual value, return them to the house, and descend deeper into a system that no longer remembers why it exists but continues to operate anyway.
